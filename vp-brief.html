Example 1 of 5 · Fuel & tankering

Tankering, read as a reviewable pattern.

The question

“How much tankering value did we actually realize this period — and how much favorable opportunity went untaken?”

What AutoPerf shows

Every plan carries an indicative tankering factor (GAIN/LOSS, in $ per tonne) whether or not extra fuel was carried. SAVINGS is recorded only when the uplift was actually taken — the one realized number. Counted one representative plan per flight, this period reads:

The decision it enables

The fuel team stops debating single flights. Where the factor is repeatedly favorable and uplift is repeatedly not taken — net of weight caps — that is a station-level policy conversation, validated afterwards against realized SAVINGS, not projections.

Read honestlyGAIN/LOSS are indicative $-per-tonne factors, not money earned. SAVINGS — recorded only when tankering was actually performed — is the sole realized figure. This brief never adds the two together.

Example 2 of 5 · Workforce & capacity

Capacity by shift, measured — with its limits stated.

The question

“How does computing demand actually sit across the day and night desks — and what does the roster hold in reserve?”

What AutoPerf shows

The Dispatcher Efficiency view works in real 12-hour Doha roster shifts (day 06:00–18:00, night 18:00–06:00 local). Working window = the rostered hours with activity. Processing = wall-clock time the optimizer was actually computing, with parallel calculations merged so they count once. This period, merging removed a — over-count that naïve summing would have reported.

The decision it enables

Leadership sees where computational workload sits across the 24-hour operation — evidence for shift balancing and surge planning before any recruitment or rostering conversation, measured the same way every week.

Read honestly“Processing” counts machine compute time only. Briefing, coordination, phone calls and monitoring never enter the log — so the utilization figure is an optimizer-utilisation indicator for capacity planning, not a measure of how busy a dispatcher is. It must not be read as a productivity scorecard.

Example 3 of 5 · Filing reliability

“Sent” is not the same as “squared away with ATC.”

The question

“The plan says it was sent to the crew. Is the flight actually complete with air traffic control?”

What AutoPerf shows

A flight can be marked sent while its ATS filing lifecycle is not fully complete — a change filed but never acknowledged, or a rejection left dangling. AutoPerf reconstructs the full lifecycle per flight and tags only clean ones Completed. This period:

The decision it enables

Gaps surface while there is still time to act — before departure, not in a call from the gate. The same filter turns weekly filing quality into one trackable number a duty manager can own.
